Our Story

Born from a personal need for connection, built for our community.

A Note from the Founder

"My name is Patrick Agaba(Cedric), born and raised in Rwanda. Over the years, I’ve become deeply concerned about the mental and emotional well-being of young people in our country. With the rise of smartphone addiction, drug use, and a growing lack of genuine human connection, many youth today are silently battling loneliness, anxiety, and emotional fatigue. In searching for a solution, I came across an inspiring article about Ilya Kneppelhout, Jordy van Bennekom, and Valentijn Klok—the founders of The Offline Club. Their story immediately resonated with me, as it aligned with the very solution I had been seeking.

"Motivated by their journey and my own experience of digital burnout and disconnection, I decided to take action. I applied to become a City Leader—and from this vision, The Kigali Offline Space was born. It's a calm and intentional environment created to bring people together for phone-free gatherings, meaningful conversations, and quiet creativity. In Kigali, where many young people are facing unemployment, stress, and depression, I truly believe this kind of space can be life-changing. I invite you to join me in building a community that values presence, connection, and mental well-being—because when we connect offline, we heal, grow, and transform the world around us."

- AGABA Patrick, Founder

Our Vision

To create a deeply connected, emotionally strong, and socially supportive youth community in Kigali.

Our Mission

To offer safe, screen-free spaces for conversation, healing, and growth, empowering young people to reconnect with themselves and each other.

Our Core Values

Connection

Real human relationships are essential for a meaningful life.

Conversation

We create spaces where people can speak openly and listen deeply.

Care

We support each other with empathy, love, and understanding.

Inclusion

Everyone is welcome, regardless of background, beliefs, or experience.

Joy

We celebrate life through laughter, games, and shared experiences.

Presence

We encourage being fully present by putting away digital distractions.
